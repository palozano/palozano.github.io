Pablo Lozano

I am Postdoctoral Fellow @Central European University where I work on networks, (explainable) AI, and computational social science.
I currently work with János Kertész, Márton Karsai, and Gerardo Iñiguez. Other coauthors are Angel Sánchez and Alberto Antonioni.

I work using modelling techniques (agent-based modelling, mainly) but I also use experimental approaches too. I make use of tools from network theory, machine learning, evolutionary game theory, and behavioral economics. I like reinforcement learning and Bayesian stats.
I did my master's in Applied Mathematics and my bachelor's in Industrial Engineering (specialization: Industrial Electronics and Robotics), both at UC3M.

Research

I'm interested in complex systems, networks, machine learning in general and reinforcement learning in particular, evolutionary game theory, and behavioral economics. Much of my research is focused on inferring behavior from human groups and understand how societies work. All the articles are open access.


Climate change uncertain decisions: an experimental approach
Pablo Lozano, Alberto Antonioni, Angel Sánchez
In preparation, 2021

Experimental approach to study which action is the best strategy when facing uncertainty in climate change.

Priming increases cooperation within competitive groups
Pablo Lozano, Alberto Antonioni, Francesca Giardini, Rafael Wittek, Siegwart Lindenberg
In preparation, 2021

Through an behavioral economics approach, we study if (verbal) priming has effect on the cooperation level within human groups.

Reinforcement learning for cooperational dynamics in uncertain reputation networks
Carlos Hernández Hernández bachelor's thesis. Co-supervised with Angel Sánchez.
July, 2021

Agent-based model to study, using reinforcement learning, how individuals learn to behave in unreliable reputation environments within dynamic networks.

Hierarchies and cooperation: an experiment
Pablo Lozano, Alberto Antonioni, Angel Sánchez
Under review, 2021

Experimental evidence showing egalitarian individuals formign hierarchies through conflicts as well as signalig of non-cooperative individuals.

Cooperation, social norm internalization, and hierarchical societies
Pablo Lozano, Sergey Gavrilets, Angel Sánchez
Scientific Reports (Nature), 2020
article  /  metrics  /  supplementary  /  bib ref

An agent-based model where a hierarchy arises from an egalitarian society, conflics can happen a social norm is internalized leading to a high cooperative (hierarchical) society.

Shall we turn off the media? Global information can destroy local cooperation in the one-dimensional ring
Bilge Aydin, Marta Biondo, Deepak Gupta, Mattia Ivaldi, Francesca Lipari, Pablo Lozano, Francesco Parino, Ennio Bilancini, Leonardo Boncinelli, Valerio Capraro
arXiv, 2019
article  /  bib ref

Numerical simulations to study the evolution of cooperation in the one-dimensional ring when the interaction structure is strictly local, and hence fitness only depends on local behaviors, while the competition structure is partly global, and hence selection can happen also between distant agents.

Cooperation on dynamic networks within an uncertain reputation environment
Pablo Lozano, Alberto Antonioni, Marco Tomassini, Angel Sánchez
Scientific Reports (Nature), 2018
article  /  metrics  /  bib ref

Agent-based model based on experimental results to study reputation evolution within a dynamic network where agents can fake reputation.
